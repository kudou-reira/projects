What is React Native?

React Native is a JavaScript framework developed by Facebook for the purpose of integrating a hybrid framework for mobile devices. As full-stack developers are already familiar with the React framework, they can directly apply their knowledge to build for both iOS and Android devices.

Previously, application engineers had to choose between Swift/Objective C for iOS and Java for Android. Now, a "one-size fits all" framework in the form of React Native simplifies the developing process. If you'd like to try out my React Native apps, you'll need an Android Phone. While React Native also has iOS flexibility, I don't have the the equipment/funds to justify uploading to the Apple App store. It costs $99 a year ;_; Maybe if I end up pursuing App development in the future, I will invest in it at that time.

I only started learning React Native a few weeks ago, but I really like developing with it because the projects I make are instantly available on my smartphone, or "スマホ." Since coming to Japan, I've come to see that Japan is a country that heavily relies on its phone, or "携帯" culture, even more so when compared with the United States. I'm always a fan of building something that I, myself, would use often. That's why the scheduling app and lastFM app were created.

The Idol Scheduling app was inspired by games such as Idolm@ster and LoveLive! In those games, you act as a "producer" that tries to manage the acitivites of various young women trying to break into the music scene. However, I tried to double down on the app to be something like a scheduling device with some texting capabilities as well. As for the LastFM application, I like to keep up with what kind of music I've been listening to. Since I already use lastFM's audioscrobbler with foobar, I felt like creating a phone app would save me a lot of time. I also want to see what my friends have been listening to as well ^^

A little bit of background info

This barebones page is to display coding projects that I have finished. The projects themselves are hosted on GitHub. My personal blog can be found here.

Before I came to Japan, I had studied Bioengineering: Premedical at the University of California, San Diego (UCSD). After receiving my Bachelor of Science degree in September 2013, I could only find jobs related to biology or biotechnology. I wasn't very interested in these kinds of jobs, so I was feeling unsatisfied. During the time from my graduation to now, I have been looking for things that are interesting such as 3D computer graphics, digital illustration, and programming. Much of these subjects are highly popular in Japan, which influenced me into coming here to learn the language and the culture.

When I was a student in high school and university, I learned the fundamentals of Objective C and Java programming. However, my degree only required me to learn the basics as it focused heavily on the engineering aspect of biotechnology. As I grew older, I came to regret the fact that I was not able to continue my studies in computer science.

Because of that, after coming to Japan, I have been learning the tools and languages necessary for web development. As of now, I have used JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, but I'm not sure if my skills are at the standard where it is useful to a company. I have worked on some projects by myself during my self-studies, so please take a look above!

In conclusion, I really like and follow things that are extremely interesting. Once I find something I like, I enthusiastically dive in. For example, towards the Japanese language, 3D computer graphics, and programming, I approach them all with a certain degree of passion because I love these subjects. In order to not feel unsatisfied like the time period after my graduation, I do my best to learn well.
